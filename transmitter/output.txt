 After Rosa Parks' arrest, many Black citizens refused to use public transportation as they made up around **70%** of the state's income from these services. Carpooling, taxi usage, and walking became more common. Following her conviction, the strike expanded further, which is known as the Montgomery Bus Boycott.

Involved taxi drivers offered rides for 10 cents (equivalent to $1.04 today), but the police interfered by setting a price of 45 cents ($4.67). This was quite expensive and led to around **300 car owners** establishing their own stations in Montgomery to pick up passengers. The movement received financial support on a national level.
